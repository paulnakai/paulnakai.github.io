I grew up in Kamuela, Hawaii, went to college in Bay Area California, and have yet to be convinced to move somewhere with real winter. I greatly enjoy board games, reading, making music, puns and general silliness, and everything that happens outside.

I tend to think, write, and talk about issues related to economic justice, socioemotional health, education, ethics, the future of work, humanism, authenticity, intentionality. I'd like to call myself a "behavioral artist" (but am resistant to the notion of personal branding). I think behavioral science is a fascinating framework for finding interesting questions to ask about the world, but art, storytelling, and clarified communication is the key to bringing us all forward, together.

Some guiding questions for me: What does it mean to do good? How do people arrive at their beliefs around good and bad? What explains the gaps between our beliefs in how to lead a good life and our behavior? What ought to be the role of work in a meaningful life?

I'm very much in that sticky, gooey, removed-from-the-oven-early emerging adulthood phase of life. I'm definitely still learning -- help me find my way!
